5-Minute Breathing Exercises for Complete Beginners

May 20, 2026 5 min read

Breathing is the only bodily function that is both automatic and voluntary. This means you have a direct line to your nervous system, and you can use it anytime, anywhere. The best part? You already know how to breathe. You just need to learn how to breathe with intention.

Why Breathing Exercises Work

When you're anxious, your breathing becomes shallow and rapid. This tells your brain there's danger, creating a feedback loop of anxiety. Slow, controlled breathing breaks this cycle by activating the vagus nerve, which triggers your body's relaxation response.

Exercise 1: Belly Breathing (2 minutes)

This is the foundation of all breathing exercises. Most of us breathe into our chests, but deep belly breathing is far more calming.

  • Place one hand on your chest and one on your belly
  • Breathe in slowly through your nose for 4 seconds
  • Feel your belly rise (your chest should stay relatively still)
  • Exhale slowly through your mouth for 6 seconds
  • Repeat for 2 minutes

Beginner's Tip

If you find it hard to breathe into your belly, try lying down. It's much easier to feel the natural belly rise when you're on your back.

Exercise 2: Box Breathing (3 minutes)

Used by Navy SEALs to stay calm under pressure, box breathing is simple and incredibly effective:

  • Inhale for 4 seconds
  • Hold for 4 seconds
  • Exhale for 4 seconds
  • Hold for 4 seconds
  • Repeat for 3 minutes (about 9 cycles)

Exercise 3: Extended Exhale (2 minutes)

The exhale is where the magic happens. A longer exhale than inhale directly stimulates your relaxation response.

  • Inhale for 3 seconds
  • Exhale for 6 seconds (try making a soft "whoosh" sound)
  • No need to hold. Just breathe in, breathe out longer
  • Continue for 2 minutes

When to Practice

  • Morning: Start your day calm with 2 minutes of belly breathing
  • Before stressful events: 1 minute of box breathing before a meeting or phone call
  • During anxiety spikes: Extended exhale breathing until you feel calmer
  • Before bed: 3-5 minutes of any technique to wind down
You don't need a special place, special equipment, or special time. You just need your breath. And you always have that with you.

Start Today

Don't try to do all three exercises at once. Pick one, practice it for a week, and notice how you feel. Consistency matters more than duration. Even 60 seconds of intentional breathing can shift your state.
